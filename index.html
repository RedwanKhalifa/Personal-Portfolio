About

My passion for technology started early. As a child, I was fascinated by gadgets, often dismantling television remotes out of sheer curiosity, much to my parents' frustration. But my real journey began in 2009 when my father brought home our first personal computer. It was unlike anything I had ever used, endlessly engaging, and full of possibilities I hadn’t imagined.

That curiosity only grew over the years. By eighth grade in 2016, I developed a strong interest in computer hardware, just as Nvidia and AMD were unveiling their next-generation graphics cards. Determined to build my own PC, I got my hands on an RX 480 and carefully assembled my first custom PC. The process was exciting, effortless, and fulfilling, making me realize that this was more than just a hobby.

That experience solidified my long-term ambition to dive deeper and learn more about computers. It set me on the path to studying Computer Engineering, bringing me to where I am today—pursuing my passion, eager to innovate, and always hungry to learn more.
